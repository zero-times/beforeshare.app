If you ever post car photos online, hiding the license plate is one of the easiest privacy upgrades you can make.

A visible number plate can identify a vehicle more precisely than most people expect. That matters on marketplace listings, in neighborhood groups, on social posts, and even in casual photos where the car is just part of the background.

## Why license plate privacy matters

People often share vehicle photos for perfectly normal reasons:

- Selling a car
- Showing a repair or modification
- Posting a travel or parking photo
- Sending a vehicle image in a chat

The risk is that the plate is still visible when the image leaves your camera roll. Once that happens, the photo can be downloaded, reposted, screenshotted, or indexed elsewhere.

## What you can do on iPhone without extra tools

There is no dedicated license plate blur feature in the iPhone Photos app.

The default workarounds are usually:

- Cropping the photo
- Covering the plate with Markup
- Manually editing the image before export

These methods are acceptable for one quick share, but they are not ideal if you post vehicle photos regularly or want a more repeatable workflow.

## Why manual editing is easy to get wrong

License plates are small details, which makes them easy to miss in:

- Wide vehicle shots
- Multiple-photo marketplace uploads
- Screenshots of listing drafts
- Mixed scenes where the car is not the main subject

Manual editing also does nothing for other risks in the same image. A car photo may include faces, QR codes, business details, or location metadata that should not be shared either.

## FAQ

### Can I blur a license plate with the iPhone Photos app?

Not with a dedicated tool. You can cover the plate manually with Markup or crop the image, but there is no built-in plate blur workflow.

### Should I hide license plates in marketplace listings?

In most cases, yes. It reduces unnecessary exposure and makes the image safer to reuse across listing sites and chats.

### Does BeforeShare upload my car photos?
